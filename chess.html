I first learned to play chess from my brother when I was in grade 2. However, I didn't start taking chess seriously until I entered high school. Since then, I have participated in several team tournaments, and have received a number of awards including first place at the TSSCL chess tournament and ranked as the 5th 12th grader in the OHSCC tournament.

Here are some resources/advice that may help. I'm not too good myself but I know a lot of friends good at chess and here are things they did to become good, in order. (None are surprising)

  • Play lots of practice games
  • Do tactics on chess.com, lichess, chesstempo
  • Become an expert in a few openings
  • Play in tournaments
  • Read books. Some good ones are:
    • My 60 Most Memorable Games by Bobby Fischer
    • Some book about endgames by Capablanca
    • Garry Kasparov on...
